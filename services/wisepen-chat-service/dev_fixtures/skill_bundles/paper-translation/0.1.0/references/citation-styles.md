# Citation Styles Quick Reference

Use these conventions when the user explicitly asks to normalize references to a specific style. Otherwise, leave references as-is.

## APA (7th)
- In-text: `(Smith & Wang, 2020)` or `Smith and Wang (2020)`
- Entry: `Smith, J., & Wang, L. (2020). Title of the paper. Journal Name, 12(3), 45–67. https://doi.org/...`
- Two authors joined by `&` in parentheses, `and` in narrative.

## MLA (9th)
- In-text: `(Smith 45)` (page number required)
- Entry: `Smith, John, and Li Wang. "Title of the Paper." Journal Name, vol. 12, no. 3, 2020, pp. 45–67.`
- Quotes around article titles; italics for journal names.

## Chicago (Author-Date)
- In-text: `(Smith and Wang 2020, 45)`
- Entry: `Smith, John, and Li Wang. 2020. "Title of the Paper." Journal Name 12 (3): 45–67.`

## IEEE
- In-text: bracketed numbers `[12]`, numbered in order of appearance.
- Entry: `[12] J. Smith and L. Wang, "Title of the paper," Journal Name, vol. 12, no. 3, pp. 45–67, 2020.`
- Use author initials + last name; no full first names.

## Common Pitfalls When Normalizing
- Do **not** re-translate author names; keep the original romanization.
- Do **not** silently drop DOIs/URLs; preserve them if present.
- If the source only gives partial info (missing page range or DOI), leave it partial — do not fabricate.
- Journal names: italics in APA/MLA/Chicago, plain in IEEE.
